The Book

Move fast and break… the timeline

A Silicon Valley startup just shipped the Bleb 1 — the world’s first consumer time machine. What could possibly go wrong?

Everything, it turns out. As the usual suspects scramble to monetize the most dangerous product ever built, three reluctant heroes are left racing to save the timeline before the bubble bursts and takes all of reality with it.

Paradox Inc. is "a skewering, hysterical satire" (NYT bestselling author Emma Brodie) about hackers, hubris, and the human cost of moving fast and breaking everything. The debut novel from Forrest Brazeal.

About

Forrest Brazeal

Paradox Inc. is my debut novel, but I’ve spent years telling stories in other forms: on stage as a keynote speaker, on the page as a writer, and one drawing at a time as a cartoonist.

I was formerly the head of developer media at Google Cloud, and I’m the co-founder of Freeman & Forrest. I also write and draw Good Tech Things, a newsletter and cartoon series for tech professionals. I live and write in Davidson, NC, where I collect old books in the forlorn hope that my kids will love them as much as I do.
